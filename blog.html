WEEK 1

Equation Solved: Time Machine = Knowledge

Published on December 1, 2025
Written by Fikri

Yes, the equation to build a time machine is solved.

Before jumping into it, I remember watching movies as a child that showed people traveling through time. It was amazing! The idea that we could see real dinosaurs in the past or flying cars in the future was magical. As Malaysians, many of us have even drawn those flying cars that were our symbol of the future.

Being born in the 90s and beyond is amazing because we had those dreams. And now, in the present, we are essentially living in that "future." While we might not have flying cars exactly as we imagined, when we look closely, we see so much progress compared to the 90s. The world has improved in countless ways.

And now, I've found a real time machine. It's been inside us all along.

Before I discovered it, there had to be an equation and I've solved it:

Time Machine = Knowledge

The derivation of the equation might sound complex, but we can actually understand it easily because it's always been a part of us. Yes, that's correct!

Before we get into the derivation, think about this. If you went back in time and changed one small thing, you could accidentally prevent your own birth! A physical time machine is a reckless, chaotic tool. Chasing that dream is a distraction from the real time travel we can all do right now.

Okay, now let's dive in: Time Machine = Knowledge.

Every machine or device developed by engineers has a specific function. Think about the transformation from the steam train to the autonomous cars we have today. The reason for this progress is always the same. We want to improve our lives, to make things better.

So, why would we want to build a time machine? What would it be for? Would you want to see the past or the future? Maybe you'd want to change it, or maybe you'd just want to visit. But I can tell you, changing the past wouldn't bring you the benefits you think it would. However, based on my equation, I can build a real time machine.

My time machine isn't made of gears and clockwork. It's made of Knowledge. When I read a biography, I'm traveling into the past to learn from someone like Einstein's mistakes. When I learn a new skill today, I'm twisting the dials to send my future self to a better destination. Knowledge is the safe, powerful, and real key to time travel.

This isn't just a philosophical idea. It's a practical tool for building a better life. Over the next few weeks, I will share the 3 pillars of creating this time machine: The Past, The Present, and The Future. We'll also explore the traditional definition of a time machine versus my philosophical definition.

But for now, I want you to start thinking like a time traveler.

If you could learn one thing from your past to improve your future, what would it be?
